STORYLINE

The Chicken Invader Z Storyline

The Dawn of the Invaders

They are an intergalactic organization of humans bent on taking over (and later on destroying) Earth Chickens for oppressing their earthly brethren. They are known for being enormous (an ordinary chicken is as big as a small spaceship), being highly technologically advanced, as proven by many of the planet-sized constructions they can make like the CK-01 Henterprise and being an overall threat to the galaxy if not the whole universe.

A Hero Emerges

THe protagonist of the story, Our saviour who has a reputation of eating space burgers with a side of sesame seeds. This trained chicken knowledgeable and experienced in martial arts and kung-fu, is the earths last hope. Will he be able to save the chickens from the Invaders or will the Chicken

Average ASAD

We know very little about his past, but he must have appeared on an unknown planet, likely in the Foreign Galaxy, eventually becoming a space faring average chicken invader and becoming what he is today. Sometime in-between, his earthly brethren have also appeared on Earth. Sometime after in the neolithic age, chickens domesticated them and started exploiting them for labour.

Terrifying TALHA

Now that the Hero stopped their invasion of the Solar System for revenge on humanity, the Chicken Invaders want to completely destroy Earth. Their plan was to secretly send Terrifying Talha behind the sun, and use it to do just that. Our hero as usual went to the local Space Burger store to order a meal. Soon after that he noticed something behind the Sun, so he flew there and found Terrifying Talha still in its construction stages. After a short confrontation Terrifying Talha wanted to destroy the Hero by shooting its eggs at him. The Hero managed to defend himself from this attack, but he also got pushed into a Black Hole in the process, which sucked him in and had him arrive at the other end of the galaxy. After that, the Chicken Invaders dispatched troops across the galaxy to stop the hero before Terrifying Talha gets finished. The hero still manages to arrive at the Solar System just before Terrifying Talha was made operational, and destroys it, ending another invasion.

Abominable AHMED

After their second defeat, the Chicken Invaders made yet another plan to destroy humanity. This time they used the Abominable Ahmed to dispatch loads of feathers to block sunlight from coming to Earth, which would result in freezing it over. It eventually arrived to the Solar System and did that just as the hero noticed this. He then confronted the Abominable Ahmed, but it flew away just as he was about to destroy it. After a bit of talking with the Narrator, the hero went to Madam Madámme, the galactic oracle, amd consulted her on how to remove the sunlight-blocking feathers. While they were talking about having to construct and use the Humanity's Greatest Fan, the Chicken Invaders overheard them and dispatched their troops to these locations where they tried to get the Artifacts before him. The hero went across the galaxy, collected all of the Artifacts and consulted Madam on constructing the device. In the meantime, he also curiously stumbled upon the Abominable Ahmed retreating, which he still didn't get to defeat. He then had to land on Earth to mount the device, with there curiously being Chickens to stop him. He used it to blow the feathers off, but it also slightly pushed the Earth away from where it was. The Abominable AHmed then came back for revenge, and the hero destroyed it and ended the invasion once again.

The Problem

No human technology can fully replace “nature’s technology”, perfected over hundreds of millions of years in delivering key services to sustain life on Earth. A productive, diverse natural world and a stable climate have been the basic assets at the foundation of the success of our civilisation, and will continue to be so in future. A fundamental issue in the previous technological revolutions has been the lightness with which we have taken for granted the natural environment rather than valuing it as a condition necessary to development.
